
Ed. Solomon

> “At the very core of this article lay the assumption of Causality,
> that the Effect cannot precede the Cause; likewise, the Aggregate
> Percentage of a Candidate cannot precede the Election Day and the
> Mail-in Percentages of that candidate. In a fair election, the
> aggregate cannot be known until after all ballots are cast; in an
> election that is unfair, where the aggregate was predetermined, the
> aggregate becomes the cause and the Mail-in Vote (and/or the Election
> Day Vote) becomes the effect…and the laws of mathematics allow us to
> readily discern between which was the cause…and which was the effect.”

> To Paraphrase Immanuel Kant: “The causation is the thing without
> which, is a condition of possibility of a thing, and so it is
> satisfied in the thing.”
